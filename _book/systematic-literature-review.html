3.2 Search Results

3.2.1 Database Searching

The systematic search of electronic databases produced 10,503 results. After removal of duplicates this was reduced to 8,709. The first author (CG) screened the title/abstract for all results. Of the 20% that were second-screened it was found that there was a strong rate of agreement 98.45% (1713/1740) and substantial rate of inter-rater reliability (\(\kappa\) = 77.6%. Following title/abstract screening there was was 325 articles remaining for full-text screening. There was 30 not available through the first author’s institution. For these studies e-mails were sent to corresponding authors to request access. This led to retrieval of a further 8 manuscripts. Study manuscripts which remained unavailable (22, 6.77%) were excluded from the review. On completion of the full-text screening process there was 126 studies remaining for inclusion in the quantiative synthesis. Second-screening at the full-text stage produced a moderate level of inter-rate reliability (\(\kappa\) = 48.86%) with agreement of 70% (21/30). Using the approaches outlined in [Additional Studies] 197 articles were identified from reference lists and forward citations searching while 99 articles were found from grey literature/provided by authors.
