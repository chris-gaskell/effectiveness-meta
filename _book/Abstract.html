The Effectiveness of Psychotherapy Outcomes in Routine Settings

Abstract

Objectives: There is now substantial practice-based evidence supporting the effectiveness of psychotherapy within naturalistic settings. The current review sought to quantitatively analyse this body of literature through meta-analysis, with a focus on moderators of outcome. Design: A systematic-review and meta-analysis protocol was pre-registered on PROSPERO (CRD42020175235). Methods: Random-effects meta-analyses were conducted on studies meeting pre-specified inclusion criteria. A range of factors that were suspected to explain heterogeneity between studies were used as moderators within meta-analyses using moderator analyses and meta-regression. Results: The systematic review identified 252 studies (298 samples), with 224 studies (265 samples) eligible for quantitative analysis. Results of the primary meta-analyses showed large effects for depression (d = 0.98, k = 140, CI [0.9-1.06], p = < 0.001), anxiety (d = 0.83, k = 83, CI [0.74-0.93], p = < 0.001), and also ‘other’ psychological outcomes (d = 1, k = 185, CI [0.93-1.07], p = < 0.001).
