3.1 Database Searching

The systematic search of electronic databases (Cinahl, Medline, PsycInfo) produced 10,503 results. After removal of duplicates with Mendeley this was reduced to 8,709. The remaining results were then imported (as .ris) to Rayyan, a web-program for managing the title and abstract screening with data-mining capabilities.

3.2 Title/Abstract Screening

The first author (CG) screened the title/abstract for all 8,709 search results using a set of pre-defined screening criteria/screening tool. In order to assess the precision of the screening process a sample of titles/abstracts (20%) was second screened by a DClinPsy trainee. Of the titles/abstracts that were double screened the agreement was 98.45% (1713/1740) while the inter-rater reliability, assessed using Cohen’s (\(\kappa\)) was 77.6%. This rate of inter-rater reliability is classified as ‘substantial’ according to the criteria outlined by Landis & Kock (1977). Following the title/abstract screening procees there was was 325 articles remaining for the full-text screening phase.

3.3 Full-Text Screening

Full-texs manuscripts that were available through the first author’s institution were retrieved for all remaining studies. There was 30 manuscripts that could not initially be accessed. For these studies e-mails were sent to corresponding authors to request access to the manuscript. This led to retrieval of a further 8 manuscripts. Study manuscripts which remained unavailable (22, 6.77%) were excluded from the review.

The screening tool used at the title/abstract stage was also used for screening full-text manuscripts. A proportion of manuscripts (10%) were screened by a second screener (Clinical Psychologist) which produced 70% (21/30) agreement and inter-rater reliability of \(\kappa\) = 48.86%. This level of inter-rater reliability was classified as ‘Moderate’ (Landis & Kock, 1977). On completion of the full-text screening process there was 126 studies remaining for inclusion in the quantiative synthesis. Table 1 shows an overview of the full-text exclusion decisions.

3.4 Additional Studies

Methods for identifying additional studies missed by the electronic database search were employed. First, the reference lists of all studies which had been full-text screening were scanned, with titles that appeared relevent to the current retreieved for review. Second we used GoogleScholar forward citation search to scan all papers that had cited the articles from the full-text screening process. Third a grey literature search using (GoogleScholar) was conducted by combining three specific search terms (“psychotherapy”, “naturalistic”, “effectiveness”) and reviewing the first 50 pages of results. Finally, the corresponding authors of the articles identified in the full-text screening process were sent a standardised e-mail to ask for any additional relvent papers that the authors knew of. Using this approach we found 197 papers from reference lists and forward citations searching and 99 papers from grey literature/author requests.
